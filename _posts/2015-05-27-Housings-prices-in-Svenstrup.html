--- layout: post title: "Housing prices in Svenstrup" date: 2015-05-27 --- So, my girlfriend and I recently became the proud owners of our first house in Svenstrup. Naturally, the process of bidding on and buying a house sparked some curiosity and thought about housing prices in Svenstrup and the 9230 zip code in general. Questions like How do prices vary between different neighborhoods? Which neighborhood is the most expensive? Are there significant differences in sq.meter prices between e.g. Svenstrup and Godthåb? came to mind. I thought a fun way to gain some insight would be to create an interactive map of housing prices which is the primary aim of this post.

First up I needed to collect some data on housing sales. Fortunately www.boliga.dk is offering search queries into the public database from SVUR (Statens Salgs- og Vurderingsregister) containing data on house sales from 1992 onwards. I performed a search on housing sales based on the following criteria
 
  • Years 1992 till 2014
  • Zipcode 9230
  • Housetype: Country house(landejendom), Villa, Apartments (ejerlejlighed), townhouse (rækkehus)
  • Ordinary free sale
I scraped and downloaded the search results using import.io's web crawler and after a bit of data cleaning I ended up with a dataset of 1565 observations/house sales within the specified period and criteria. Note that there is a discrepancy between the number of search results returned by Boliga and the number of sales extracted by the web crawler and the 1565 extracted observations will roughly represent about 85% of the actual number of sales in the period (I didn't bother tweaking the scraper or trying other tools to increase performance). The dataset contains variables Address, Price, Date of sale, Type of sale, Price pr. sq.meter, Number of rooms, Sq.meter size, Housing type, Year of construction, Price reduction in percent. Moreover, the data only contains the latest sale e.g. if Jættestuen 17 were sold in both 1998 and 2006 then only the 2006 sale is included in the data

So the sales data is secured but the goal is to plot these sales on an interactive map. The next step is therefore to collect latitude and longitude coordinates for each address in the sales data in order plot them on a map. The Danish Ministry of Housing, Urban and Rural Affairs (Ministeriet for by, Bolig og Lansddistrikter) maintains a database of all addresses in Denmark where each address comes with corresponding latitude and longitude coordinates assigned. The adresses for each of the 98 municipalties and 5 regions can be found here. By merging the addresses and the sales dataset we are able to map each sale based on latitude and longitude coordinates using R and Christian Grauls @chgrl LeafletR package.

2014-price


A bit of data wrangling and the map above magically appears. Each sale is adjusted for inflation and calculated in 2014 prices. Note that the calculated 2014-price for a given house sold in e.g. 1995 isn't an actual prediction of the present value of that house. Obviously, a lot of other factors affects housing prices, like home improvements for example. This means that the calculated 2014-prices solely illustrates what a given home sold in 1995 would cost in 2014-prices keeping all other things equal. Every sale is categorized into percentiles based on the 2014-price e.g. 90-100% represents the top 10% most expensive sales in 2014-prices and 25-75% represents the middle 50% of all sales based on 2014-prices.

While the map above shows absolute prices it could be equally interesting to map the 2014-prices pr. squaremeter. But not surprisingly, the two maps differ only sligthy and in order to keep this post short I decided to leave out the map of squaremeter prices. You can click each datapoint to see the price pr. squaremeter for each sale.

Zooming in on Svenstrup/Godthåb, a visual inspection reveals clear clusters of red dots centered around Svenstruphøj, Asgården, Midgården, Lunden and Birkagervænget. This suggest that these are the most expensive neighborhoods in Svenstrup/Godthåb measured in 2014-prices.Unfortunately, the data provided by Boliga doesn't let you identify building plots, but by using the sales date and the built date it's possible to subset the data and get a fairly accurate idea of where the building plots were located within the depicted period. It turns out that pretty much every building plot in Svenstrup/Godthåb is located in exactly the five neighborhoods mentioned above - meaning that it's the newly established neighborhoods that are raking in the highest prices. Moreover, the large majority of building plots in the entire 9230 zip code were located in Svenstrup or Godthåb, which might suggest that out of the five cities in 9230 these two are experiencing the most growth (building plots are removed from the map above).
